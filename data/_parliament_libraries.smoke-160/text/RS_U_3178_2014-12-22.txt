Lifts for disabled students (a) whether it is a fact that there is no system of lifts for disabled students in Government schools in the country;&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(b) if so, the details thereof; &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(c) whether Government proposes to issue instructions to all concerned in the country for provision of lifts for disabled students in Government run institutes/ schools to help them to pursue their courses with comfort; and&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(d) if so, the details thereof? MINISTER OF HUMAN RESOURCE DEVELOPMENT&lt;br/&gt;(SMT. SMRITI ZUBIN IRANI)&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(a) to (d): Under Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA) & Rashtriya Madhymic Shiksha Abhiyan (RMSA), there is no specific provision for the lifts in Government Schools for disabled children. However, under SSA, States and UT’s are assisted to enrol children with special needs (CWSN) in schools, through early identification, assessment camps, distribution of aids & appliances, appropriate teaching learning equipments, training of teachers in inclusive strategies, barrier free school infrastructure including ramps with railings as well as home based learning methods. &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;The RMSA also provides barrier free access to disabled children at secondary level. &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;The University Grant Commission (UGC) has implemented a scheme called “Higher education for Persons with Special needs (HEPSN) which is basically meant for creating an environment at the higher education institutions to enrich higher education learning experiences for differently-abled persons. Creating awareness about the capabilities of differently-abled persons, constructions aimed at improving accessibility, purchase of equipments to enrich learning etc. are the broad categories of assistance under the scheme.
