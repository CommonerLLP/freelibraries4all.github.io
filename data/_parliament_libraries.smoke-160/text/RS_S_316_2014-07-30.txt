Pathetic condition of ASI maintained museums GOVERNMENT OF INDIA&lt;br/&gt;MINISTRY OF CULTURE&lt;br/&gt;RAJYA SABHA&lt;br/&gt;STARRED QUESTION NO. 316&lt;br/&gt;TO BE ANSWERED ON 30.07.2014&lt;br/&gt;SRAVANA 8, 1936 (SAKA)&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;PATHETIC CONDITION OF ASI MAINTAINED MUSEUMS&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;*316. SHRI VIJAY JAWAHARLAL DARDA:&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Will the Minister of CULTURE be pleased to state:&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(a) whether Government is aware that most of the museums maintained by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) are in a very pathetic condition;&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(b) how many museums are being maintained by ASI along with names and how many visitors visit these museums, the museum-wise and year-wise details thereof for the last three years;&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(c) whether many museums lack any notable artifacts and for those artifacts that are there, most descriptions are absent; and&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(d) if so, what is being done by Government to make these museums educative for children, students and foreign visitors? &lt;br/&gt;ANSWER&lt;br/&gt;MINISTER OF STATE (IC) CULTURE & TOURISM&lt;br/&gt;(SHRI SHRIPAD NAIK)&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(a) to (d) A statement is laid on the table of the House&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt; &lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;STATEMENT REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (a) to (d) OF THE RAJYA SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 316 FOR 30.07.2014&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(a) No, Sir. All the Archaeological Site Museums under ASI are in presentable condition. However, maintenance of museum is a regular nature of work and accordingly all the museums are being maintained.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(b) Details are at Annexure - I (pp.3-4).&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(c) No, Sir. The Archaeological Site Museums are mostly located inside monument(s) or adjacent to the excavated site(s) to keep the small and movable antiquities, recovered from the ancient site, in close association with the remains to which they belong.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;The representative/ prominent antiquities are displayed within the available space in the museum’s galleries. These displayed objects are mostly supported by bilingual or trilingual captions/ labels and narrations, touch screen kiosk, virtual walk through, Braille signages, etc. for the easy understanding of sites and objects/ antiquities. However, write-ups are replaced/ updated as and when required.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;(d) The Archaeological Site Museums of ASI are being emphasized on interactive and storytelling labels for easy understanding by children/ students and foreign visitors. Apart from that, the museums also organize cultural awareness programme on the various occasion such as on World Heritage Day, World Heritage week, International Museum Day, Children’s Day, Independence Day, Republic Day, etc.&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Annexure - I&lt;br/&gt;ANNEXURE REFERRED TO IN REPLY TO PART (b) OF RAJYA SABHA STARRED QUESTION NO. 316 &lt;br/&gt;FOR 30.07.2014&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Details of visitors in Archaeological Site Museums under ASI during the Last Three Years&lt;br/&gt;&lt;br/&gt;Sl.
